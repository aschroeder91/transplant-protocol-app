# ICU Clinical Pathway (POD 0)
- Admission from OR to ICU (DCD, ECD, or unstable SCD).
- Induction: ATG 1.5 mg/kg IV over 6 hours or Basiliximab 20 mg IV.
- Premedication for ATG: Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV, acetaminophen 650 mg, diphenhydramine 25–50 mg.
- Continuous monitoring: vitals q15min x1hr, q30min x4hr, then q4hr; telemetry.
- Foley catheter for hourly UOP; goal >0.5 mL/kg/hr.
- Initial labs: CBC, BMP, LFTs, PT/INR, Uric acid, Tac trough (if previously transplanted).
- IV fluids: NS or ½ NS at 100–150 mL/hr; titrate based on CVP/UOP.
- Pain control: Fentanyl infusion or scheduled acetaminophen (adjust per renal function).
- Imaging: Doppler renal US within 6–12 hours.

# POD 1
- Vitals q4hr, neuro and fluid status checks.
- Labs: CBC, BMP, Mg, Phos, Tac, LFTs.
- Begin oral Tac (0.05–0.1 mg/kg BID), MMF 1–1.5g BID, Prednisone 20 mg PO.
- Prophylaxis: TMP-SMX DS MWF, Valganciclovir (dose per eGFR), Nystatin 5 mL PO QID.
- IV fluids matched to UOP; taper as PO improves.
- Reinforce education on meds, hygiene, follow-up.

# POD 2–3
- Remove Foley if ambulating, tolerating PO.
- Daily weight, I/O, and volume status reassessment.
- Continue immunosuppressants, monitor troughs and adjust.
- Encourage ambulation, bowel regimen (Senna + Docusate).

# POD 4–5
- Evaluate for DGF: Cr trend, urine output.
- Order renal scan or biopsy if function abnormal.
- Ensure medication access, education reviewed with pharmacy.

# Discharge Day
- Stable Cr/UOP, PO meds tolerated, education complete.
- All consults (pharmacy, social work, dietary) documented.
- Discharge summary, meds, and follow-up provided to patient.
