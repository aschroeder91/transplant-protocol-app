# PACU Protocol for Kidney Transplant Recipient

## Patient Flow
- OR -> PACU (hemodynamic stabilization + ultrasound) -> 10 Mountain ICU (overnight) -> 10 South

## Purpose
- Maintain hemodynamic stability, optimize intravascular volume and graft perfusion, and confirm graft viability prior to ICU transfer following kidney transplant.
- Target PACU stay: less than 2 hours.

## Goal
- Maintain MAP >= 70 mmHg to protect graft perfusion.
- Optimize fluid balance.
- Confirm graft viability, then transfer to ICU.

## I. Immediate PACU Priorities
- Maintain adequate blood pressure to ensure graft perfusion.
- Optimize fluid balance.
- Monitor urine output.
- Identify early vascular or surgical complications.
- Complete post-operative kidney ultrasound prior to ICU transfer.

## II. Hemodynamic Monitoring and Blood Pressure Goals
- Continuous cardiac monitoring and pulse oximetry.
- Arterial line monitoring preferred.

### Blood Pressure Targets
- Goal MAP >= 70 mmHg.
- Minimum MAP >= 65 mmHg.
- Target systolic BP 120-160 mmHg.

### Notify transplant surgery immediately for
- MAP < 65 mmHg.
- Systolic BP < 100 mmHg.
- Hypotension associated with decreased urine output.
- Escalating vasopressor requirements.

## III. Vital Signs and Monitoring
- Vital signs every 15 minutes x 1 hour.
- Then every 30 minutes x 2 hours.
- Continuous SpO2 and cardiac monitoring.

## IV. Lines, Tubes, and Devices
Kidney transplant recipients are expected to arrive to PACU with:
- Central venous catheter.
- Arterial line.
- Urine-measuring Foley catheter (Sensica).
- Surgical JP drain.

- Lines and monitoring devices should remain in place unless directed by the transplant team.

## V. Intake and Output
- Strict intake and output monitoring.
- Hourly urine output via Foley catheter.

### Notify transplant surgery for
- Urine output < 50 mL/hr (if producing urine).
- Sudden decrease in urine output (if producing urine).

## VI. Physical Assessment
- Abdominal assessment for distention, tenderness, or firmness.
- Surgical incision and drain assessment.
- Foley catheter patency and urine color assessment.
- Do not flush Foley catheter.

## VII. Ultrasound
A post-operative kidney transplant ultrasound is required in PACU prior to ICU transfer to confirm:
- Graft perfusion.
- Renal artery patency.
- Renal vein patency.
- Absence of early surgical complications.

- The circulating RN will notify ultrasound technician to meet in PACU.
- The ultrasound should be completed on arrival to PACU.
- Abnormal findings require immediate notification of transplant surgery.
- Vascular surgery will read and document the ultrasound.
- Once ultrasound is complete, labs are drawn, and the patient is hemodynamically stable, the patient can be transferred to ICU.

## VIII. Laboratory Monitoring
Labs drawn on PACU arrival and as ordered:
- CBC.
- BMP.
- Magnesium.
- Phosphorus.
- Tacrolimus level (if initiated intraoperatively).

### Notify transplant surgery for
- Rising creatinine.
- Hyperkalemia.
- Significant anemia or leukopenia.

## IX. Fluid Management
- IV fluids per transplant protocol (LR).
- Hourly urine output replacement plus 50%.
- Avoid giving fluid boluses. Additional fluid modification needs to be addressed with surgeon.
- Monitor closely for signs of volume overload, including pulmonary edema.

### Early symptoms of volume overload
- New or increasing oxygen requirement.
- Patient reporting feeling short of breath.
- Tachypnea (RR > 20).
- Crackles or rales at lung bases.
- Feelings of anxiety or restlessness.
- Decreasing SpO2 despite stable BP.

## X. Pain and Medication Management
- Multimodal analgesia preferred.
- Avoid NSAIDs and Toradol.
- Antiemetics as needed.

## XI. Complications and Emergency Management
- Bleeding: notify transplant surgery, assess labs, transfuse as indicated, prepare for possible return to OR.
- Hypotension: IV fluids, vasopressors as needed, reassess graft perfusion.
- No urine output: verify Foley patency, assess perfusion, consider diuretics, obtain Doppler ultrasound.
- Suspected vascular thrombosis: STAT Doppler ultrasound and emergent transplant surgery notification.

## XII. Communication and Transfer
Early notification of transplant surgery is required for:
- Decreased urine output.
- Hemodynamic instability.
- Abnormal laboratory or ultrasound findings.

Clear handoff to ICU nursing must include:
- Blood pressure and MAP trends.
- Fluid replacement strategy.
- Urine output trends.
- Ultrasound results.
- Pending labs or ongoing concerns.
- Time of last lab draw.
