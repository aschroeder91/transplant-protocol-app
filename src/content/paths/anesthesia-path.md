# Kidney Transplant - Anesthesia Protocol

## I. Lines and Monitoring
- ASA standard monitors: ECG, SpO2, ETCO2, temperature, BP.
- Consider arterial line based on patient comorbidities and anticipated fluid shifts.
- Large-bore peripheral IV x2 minimum.
- Central line optional based on patient comorbidities.
- Patients receiving Thymoglobulin for immunosuppression should receive a central line.

## II. Induction and Maintenance
Anesthetic agents and techniques are at the discretion of the anesthesia provider, based on patient comorbidities, hemodynamic goals, and clinical judgment. Listed below are potential medications to consider, and providers should use caution with renally excreted medications.

### Induction
- Midazolam 1-2 mg IV (optional).
- Fentanyl 100 mcg IV.
- Etomidate or Propofol.
- Rocuronium, Vecuronium, or Cisatricurium.

### Maintenance
- Inhalation anesthetics and/or propofol infusions.
- Multimodal pain control may include narcotics, acetaminophen, ketamine, and dexmedetomidine.
- High-dose steroids used for immunosuppression also contribute to multimodal pain control.
- Avoid NSAIDs and morphine.
- Maintain MAP > 75 mmHg.
- Maintain normothermia.
- Foley catheter with urometer placed post-induction.
- Label and document all lines and tubes in EPIC.

### Emergence
- Plan for extubation in OR.
- Ensure paralysis is fully reversed. Literature overall supports Sugammadex as a safe option.
- Consider deep extubation when appropriate for theoretical protection of vascular anastomoses.

## III. Fluids and Intraoperative Drugs
- Balanced crystalloids preferred (Plasmalyte, LR).
- Avoid nephrotoxic drugs.
- Mannitol 12.5 g IV pre-reperfusion.
- Lasix 20 mg IV PRN for poor urine output.
- Routine use of Albumen is discouraged due to lack of clear efficacy evidence.
- Avoid starch-based volume expanders.

## IV. Induction Immunosuppression and Steroid (Pre-Reperfusion)

### Medication Protocol
- Solu-Medrol: 500 mg, IV push, pre-incision.
- Basiliximab: 20 mg, IV, at induction.
- Thymoglobulin: 1.5 mg/kg, IV over 6 hours, at induction.
- Mycophenolate: 1000 mg, PO/IV, pre-op if not NPO.
- Tacrolimus: 0.05 mg/kg BID, PO, hold until POD1 unless ordered.

- Immunosuppression is given based on ideal body weight.

### Low Risk (all criteria must be met) - Basiliximab
- First kidney transplant.
- Calculated panel reactive antibody (cPRA) < 20%.
- No donor-specific antibodies (DSA).
- Negative crossmatch.

### High Risk (any criteria) - Thymoglobulin
- cPRA >= 20%.
- Presence of any DSA.
- Positive or indeterminate crossmatch.
- Repeat kidney transplant.
- Deceased donor transplant.

## V. Intraoperative Medication Timing
- Heparin: 3,000 units IV, before clamping vessels.
- Mannitol: 12.5 g IV, approximately 10 minutes before reperfusion.
- Lasix: 20 mg IV, if poor urine output at reperfusion.
- Cefazolin: 2 g IV, within 30 minutes pre-incision; redose every 4 hours.
- Immunosuppressants: per protocol, before reperfusion.

## VI. Hemodynamic Targets
- Maintain MAP >= 75 mmHg throughout the procedure.

### Reperfusion Targets
- Living donor transplant: MAP >= 75 mmHg.
- Deceased donor transplant: MAP >= 80 mmHg for first 10-15 minutes post-reperfusion.

### Vasoactive Support
- Norepinephrine:
- Start 0.02-0.05 mcg/kg/min IV infusion.
- Typical range 0.02-0.2 mcg/kg/min.
- Titrate to MAP target, not urine output.
- Vasopressin:
- 0.03 units/min IV infusion (fixed).
- Use as adjunct when norepinephrine is insufficient.
- Inotropes only if low cardiac output is documented:
- Dobutamine 2-10 mcg/kg/min IV.
- Epinephrine 0.01-0.05 mcg/kg/min IV.
